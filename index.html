According to the Continuous Update Project from the American Institute for Cancer Research, there is convincing evidence that red meat is a cause of colorectal cancer and cardiovasuclar disease.

This means hamburgers, pork chops, bacon and steaks are best enjoyed in smaller portions, a few times a day. The AICR recommends eating no more than 18oz of red meat weekly.

What is considered red meat?

  • Beef
  • Veal
  • Pork
  • Lamb
  • Mutton
  • Goat
  • Horse
  • Ostrich*

Becoming a vegetarian or vegan is not necessary – there are plenty of ways to cut down your red meat intake if you are in the danger zone.

Good substitutions include:

  • Fish
  • Chicken
  • Turkey
  • Nuts
  • Beans
  • Whole grains


Replacing one serving a day of red meat with one of these options can reduce mortality by 7% to 19%.
